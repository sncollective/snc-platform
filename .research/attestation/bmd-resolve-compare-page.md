# Attestation: DaVinci Resolve Free vs Studio Compare Page (Blackmagic Design)

## Paraphrased summary

The compare page provides a high-level differentiation between DaVinci Resolve Free and Studio. The free version is described as working with "virtually all 8-bit video formats at up to 60fps in resolutions as high as Ultra HD 3840×2160" and including multi-user collaboration and HDR grading. Studio adds everything in the free version plus the DaVinci AI Neural Engine, dozens of additional Resolve FX, temporal and AI spatial noise reduction, text-based editing, magic mask, film grain, optical blur, 10-bit video support up to 120fps and resolutions beyond 4K, stereoscopic 3D tools, professional HDR grading (Dolby Vision, HDR10+), and immersive 3D audio.

The compare page does NOT explicitly enumerate scripting/automation, network media, MAM integration, headless rendering, or interchange format support in the visible comparison rows. The scripting differentiation is stated more explicitly on the Studio product page (see bmd-resolve-studio-product-page).

## Key passages

> "DaVinci Resolve (Free): Works with virtually all 8-bit video formats at up to 60fps in resolutions as high as Ultra HD 3840 x 2160. Includes multi-user collaboration and HDR grading."

> "DaVinci Resolve Studio ($295): includes everything in the free version plus the DaVinci AI Neural Engine, dozens of additional Resolve FX, temporal and AI spatial noise reduction, text based editing, magic mask, film grain, optical blur and more. Supports 10-bit video up to 120 frames per second and resolutions beyond 4K."
