# US Copyright Office — Section 512 Study

## Paraphrased Summary

The US Copyright Office's study of Section 512 of the DMCA, confirming the notice-and-takedown framework and safe harbor requirements for hosting providers.

## What Qualifies as a Hosting Service Provider

Hosting providers fall under the category of OSPs that "store material at the direction of a user on a service provider's system or network," such as "websites that host user-generated content."

## Notice-and-Takedown Procedure

When a rightsholder sends a takedown notice, the provider must "act expeditiously to remove, or disable access to" the disputed material. The notice must contain substantially:
- Identification of the copyrighted work
- Location of the infringing material
- Rightsholder contact information
- Statement under penalty of perjury that the sender is authorized and believes the use is infringing

## Counter-Notice Process

If a user disputes removal, they may submit a counter-notice asserting the material was removed "as a result of mistake or misidentification." The provider must then restore access within 10–14 business days, unless the original notice sender files a court action.

## Designated Agent Requirement

Providers must "designate an agent to receive DMCA takedown notices" and register with the Copyright Office. Must also publish the agent's name, address, phone, and email "in a location accessible to the public."

## Repeat Infringer Policy

All providers must "adopt and implement a policy that results in account termination for repeat infringers," including users with multiple removed items regardless of court findings.

## What Jeopardizes Safe Harbor

Providers lose protection if they:
- Receive actual knowledge or "awareness of facts or circumstances from which infringing activity is apparent" and fail to act
- "Receive a financial benefit directly as a result of infringing activity" while controlling it
