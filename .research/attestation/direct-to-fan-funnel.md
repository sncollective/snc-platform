# Direct-to-fan email→subscription funnel pattern (engaged via search summary — reduced substrate)

## Paraphrased summary

The prevailing direct-to-fan playbook: an artist's email list is the **owned audience layer** that funnels fans into a **single primary fan-funding home** (Bandcamp / Patreon / Substack / Ko-fi) where recurring revenue lives. The newsletter/email layer drives traffic into the funding platform; you don't try to monetize inside the email tool itself.

## Key passages (as reported by the search summary — NOT source-direct)

- "The cleaner pattern is to pick one fan-funding platform as the primary subscription home, then use a newsletter … as the email layer that drives traffic into it."
- Reported (uncorroborated) funnel figures: ~5–10% of followers convert to email subscribers; a well-nurtured list converts ~15–25% to paying on a well-positioned offer. **Search-summary-reported; not verified at source.**
- Bandcamp framed as catalog/release-event oriented; Patreon as ongoing-relationship oriented; many artists use both.

## Disconfirming / reliability note
