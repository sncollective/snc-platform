# DMCA Section 512 — Online Copyright Infringement Liability Limitation Act

## Paraphrased Summary

Section 512 of the DMCA (the Online Copyright Infringement Liability Limitation Act) creates safe harbor provisions for online service providers hosting user-generated content. The §512(c) provision is the most relevant for UGC video platforms.

## Four Safe Harbor Provisions

**§512(a) — Transitory Communications:** Protects providers acting as passive conduits when infringing material passes through automatically without modification, stored temporarily.

**§512(b) — System Caching:** Shields OSPs who create copies for faster access via standard methods without interfering with copy protection.

**§512(c) — Storage:** Covers OSPs hosting user-uploaded material. Most commonly invoked — applies to platforms like YouTube that host UGC video.

**§512(d) — Information Location Tools:** Protects search engines linking to infringing content where they lack knowledge of infringement.

## Requirements for §512(c) Storage Safe Harbor

Three core conditions:

1. **Financial Benefit Test:** Not receive financial benefit "directly attributable to the infringing activity." Courts examine whether infringing content drives user acquisition.

2. **Knowledge Standard:** Neither have actual knowledge of infringement nor "be aware of facts or circumstances from which infringing activity is apparent" (the "red flag" test).

3. **Response Obligation:** "Act expeditiously to remove or disable access" upon receiving proper notice.

## General Eligibility Requirements §512(i) — All Safe Harbors

- Implement and reasonably enforce a repeat infringer termination policy
- Accommodate standard technical measures for copyright protection
- Inform users of infringer policies

## Notice-and-Takedown Process

**Proper Notice must include:**
- Authorized signature
- Identification of copyrighted work
- Location of infringing material
- Contact details
- Good-faith belief statement
- Accuracy affirmation under penalty of perjury

**Designated Agent:** OSPs must appoint and register a designated agent with the Copyright Office.

**Takedown Timeline:** Requires "expeditious" removal (not a specific number of days).

**Counter-Notice:** Alleged infringers may submit counter-notices (under penalty of perjury) claiming mistaken takedown; 10–14 business days before reinstatement, unless copyright holder files suit.

**Repeat Infringer Policy:** Account termination required for users with multiple removals regardless of court findings.

## Key Passages

> "Act expeditiously to remove or disable access" upon proper notice.

> The knowledge standard: "be aware of facts or circumstances from which infringing activity is apparent" — the "red flag" test.

## Structural Notes

The article notes a common misconception that "ten days are required" for takedown — that period actually applies to counter-notice procedures after removal, not initial takedown.
