# iubenda — Does GDPR require double opt-in?

## Paraphrased summary

Double opt-in (confirm-via-emailed-link before adding to a list) is **not legally mandated by GDPR**, though it is widely considered best practice in the EU (especially Germany). What GDPR actually requires is provable, unambiguous consent — which double opt-in alone does not satisfy; the controller must also collect explicit consent (a checkbox), link the privacy policy, and keep a record of when/how consent was obtained and what the subject was told.

## Key passages

- "No, there's no requirement under GDPR to have a double opt-in process." It is "considered best practice in many countries, especially Germany and in the EU."
- Valid consent additionally requires: **checkbox fields with consent clauses**, **a link to your privacy policy**, and **proof of consent documentation**.
- "double opt-in on its own doesn't guarantee GDPR compliance because it's not enough to prove consent" — you must "keep a record of these consents" and "be able to provide proof of when and how you got consent and what they were told."
