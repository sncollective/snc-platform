# QR-at-shows fan-capture pattern (engaged via search summary — reduced substrate)

## Paraphrased summary

A recurring industry pattern for capturing fan contacts at live shows: place a **QR code in the venue** tied to an incentive (free merch, signed item, exclusive content / prize), fans scan → a 1-click signup captures email/phone, and the contacts route into a fan-CRM (e.g. Laylo) for post-show engagement. A common QR-driver tool named is **Set.live**, paired with Laylo as the destination list.

## Key passages (as reported by the search summary — NOT source-direct)

- Artists place QR codes in venues that fans scan "to win prizes like signed postcards or merch bundles," routing the contacts back to a fan platform.
- A QR-driver tool, **Set.live**, is described as free and quick to set up; contacts link to fan platforms like Laylo.
- Reported (uncorroborated) figures: "70% of fan list growth happens during tour cycles" and "75% of all data capture on Laylo comes from drops tied to live shows." **These are search-summary-reported vendor figures; not verified at source.**
