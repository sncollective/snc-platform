# Content Moderation — Wikipedia

## Paraphrased Summary

Wikipedia's article on content moderation covering approaches, methods, and community governance models for managing user-generated content on platforms.

## Pre-Moderation vs Post-Moderation

The article does not use these terms explicitly but describes:

**Proactive approaches:** Platforms use "algorithmic tools, user reporting and human review" to identify problems before widespread distribution.

**Reactive moderation:** "When users are faced with an image or video they deem unfit, they can click the report button." Users of a platform report content that is inappropriate.

## Automated vs Human Review

Modern platforms use "a combination of algorithmic tools, user reporting and human review." Companies increasingly rely on artificial intelligence to screen graphic content, though "critics claim that it is insufficient." Human moderators remain essential for nuanced judgment on context and intent.

## UGC-Specific Challenges

Content moderators must assess user-generated material for relevance, obscenity, legality, and harm. Specific challenges include:
- Revenge porn
- Graphic violence
- Conspiracy theories
- Material targeting minors

Requires both technical filtering and human contextual understanding.

## Community Governance Models

**Supervisor moderation:** Appointed moderators with special privileges enforce policies.

**User moderation:** Community members moderate each other through voting and reporting mechanisms, leveraging "the votes of the rest of the community" to screen problematic contributions.

## Key Passages

> "A combination of algorithmic tools, user reporting and human review" is the standard multi-method approach.

> "Supervisor moderation" vs "user moderation" as the two primary community governance patterns.
