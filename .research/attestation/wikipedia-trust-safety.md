# Trust and Safety — Wikipedia

## Paraphrased Summary

Wikipedia's article on Trust and Safety as a platform organizational function, covering its scope, methods, and the relationship between automated and human moderation.

## Definition

Trust and Safety (T&S) refers to "organizational functions, teams, policies, and technologies that online platforms use to protect users from harmful content, abusive behavior, fraud, and security threats." Emerged from e-commerce contexts in the late 1990s.

## Key Areas

- Account integrity and fraud prevention
- Content moderation across user-generated material
- Child safety and exploitation prevention
- Platform manipulation detection
- Regulatory compliance

## Pre- vs Post-Moderation Pattern

The article describes platforms using "a combination of automated systems and human reviewers to enforce content policies at scale":
1. **Pre-moderation:** Prevention before the content is live
2. **Proactive post-moderation:** Scanning after content goes live
3. **Reactive post-moderation:** Response to user reports

This three-window model is the documented pattern for major platforms.

## Implementation Methods

Dual-track systems:
- **Automated detection:** Machine learning, hash-matching technologies, natural language processing
- **Human review:** Professional moderators and outsourced teams applying policy guidelines

Appeals and context-dependent enforcement frameworks accommodate user intent and cultural considerations.

## Key Passage

> "A combination of automated systems and human reviewers to enforce content policies at scale before the content is live, proactive review after the content is live, and reactive review after the content is live."
