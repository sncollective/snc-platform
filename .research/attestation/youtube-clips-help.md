# Attestation: YouTube Clips Help Page

## Summary

YouTube's official help article covers how viewers and creators interact with the Clips feature. The page addresses clip creation, length constraints, creator controls (enable/disable), discoverability surfaces, live stream behavior, and content removal conditions. It is the primary user-facing specification document for YouTube Clips.

## Key passages and facts

### Who can create clips

The page establishes that "Clipping videos is turned on by default," implying any viewer can clip eligible videos. No subscriber count threshold for clip creation eligibility is stated on this page. No eligibility threshold for the viewing account is stated either.

### Clip length limits

"You can increase or decrease the length of your selection to a maximum of 60 seconds or minimum of 5 seconds." The selection UI uses a draggable slider.

### Clip visibility

"Clips are public and can be watched and shared by anyone with access to it, provided they can also watch the original video." Clips from unlisted videos "remain available" even when the source is unlisted.

### Clip removal conditions

Clips become unavailable if:
- The original video is deleted or set to private
- The original video violates Community Guidelines

Clips made from unlisted videos remain available.

### Live stream clips

"You can't create Clips from live streams without DVR or live streams longer than the DVR timeframe." Clips from live streams "appear only after streaming concludes and uploads as video" — meaning live-stream clips are available only after the stream ends and the VOD is available.

### Creator controls — disabling clips

Exact disable steps from the page:
1. Sign in to YouTube Studio
2. From the left Menu, click Settings
3. Select Channel → Advanced Settings
4. Under Clips, uncheck "Allow viewers to clip my content"
5. Click Save

A creator can also add specific users to a "Hidden users" list to prevent a particular person from clipping. Blocked words can prevent certain terms in clip titles.

### Discovery surfaces

Clips appear on:
- The "Clips library" (accessible through the app's "You" tab under "Your Clips")
- "Search, discovery, and analytics surfaces"
- An optional "Top community clips" shelf on channel homepages — added via YouTube Studio → Customization → new section → "Top community clips." Clips on this shelf are "publicly visible and organized by popularity and how recently they were created." The shelf can be repositioned by dragging.

### Creator management of viewer clips

Creators can view clips made from their content through YouTube Studio. Actions available on viewer-created clips: share, play, "hide user from channel," or "report clip." The page does not state that creators can directly delete viewer-made clips — only clips the creator themselves made can be deleted.

### Attribution and clip page

The page does not describe what text or attribution appears on the clip viewing page itself. Creators can see "who made it" in YouTube Studio clip analytics — meaning clipper identity is visible to the creator in Studio but the page does not specify public-facing display on the clip page.

### Shorts integration

"Creators can remix clips into Shorts if the source video is eligible for remix. Clip creators can convert an entire clip into a Short."

### Clip analytics for creators

Under YouTube Studio → Clips section: creators can view clip performance metrics including view counts per clip, clipper identity, and clip details.
